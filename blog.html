Cost of Living for International Students in Malaysia 2026

📅 May 28, 2026 ✍️ EduPath Research Team ⏱ 6 min read

Malaysia is consistently ranked as one of the most affordable countries for international students in Asia. But knowing the exact numbers before you arrive is essential for planning your finances with confidence. This guide breaks down every single expense category based on 2026 data.

💡 Quick Summary: A typical international student in Kuala Lumpur spends between RM 1,800–3,500 per month all-in — including rent, food, transport, and personal expenses. In Johor Bahru or Penang, expect RM 1,400–2,800.

🏠 Accommodation

Accommodation is usually your largest single expense. Here's what to expect across different options:

🍜 Food

Malaysia is a food paradise with incredibly affordable eating options. Local hawker stalls, mamak restaurants and food courts can feed you well for very little:

  • Hawker meal (nasi lemak, mee goreng, etc.): RM 4–8
  • Mamak restaurant meal + drink: RM 7–14
  • Cafeteria meal at university: RM 5–9
  • Mid-range restaurant: RM 20–40 per meal
  • Grocery shopping (monthly): RM 200–350
  • Average food budget: RM 400–700/month

🚌 Transport

Kuala Lumpur has an extensive public transport network including the LRT, MRT, Monorail, KTM and Rapid KL buses. As a student, you can get discounted MyRapid cards:

  • Monthly unlimited MyRapid Card (KL): RM 100–150
  • Grab (ride-hailing) — occasional trips: RM 50–120/month
  • If you have a car/motorbike: RM 150–300/month (fuel + parking)
  • Average transport budget: RM 100–200/month

📱 Utilities & Internet

  • Mobile SIM with data (Celcom/Maxis/Digi/U Mobile): RM 35–80/month
  • Home internet (fibre, shared): RM 30–60/month
  • Electricity + water (if not included in rent): RM 60–120/month

🏥 Health Insurance

Most Malaysian universities require international students to have health insurance. The cost is usually bundled into your enrollment fees:

  • University-provided health plan: RM 400–800/year
  • Private plan (more comprehensive): RM 800–2,000/year

Can International Students Work While Studying in Malaysia?

📅 May 15, 2026 ✍️ EduPath Legal Team ⏱ 5 min read

Yes — international students in Malaysia are legally allowed to work part-time while studying, but with specific restrictions. Understanding these rules is critical to avoid visa violations.

✅ Key Rule: International students may work up to 20 hours per week during semester, and full-time during official semester breaks, provided they are enrolled at an approved private higher education institution (PHEI).

📋 Who Is Eligible?

  • You must be enrolled at a recognised private university or college
  • You must hold a valid Student Pass (eVAL / VAL)
  • Your university must have approved part-time employment status for you
  • You must work only in permitted sectors (see below)

✅ Permitted Sectors for Part-Time Work

  • Restaurants and food service
  • Mini markets and retail
  • Petrol stations
  • Hotels (non-nightclub positions)
  • Family businesses

❌ Restricted Sectors

  • Casinos or gaming establishments
  • Pubs, nightclubs, or bars
  • Health spas or massage parlours
  • Any sector violating Malaysian law

💡 Practical Earning Potential

The minimum wage in Malaysia is currently RM 1,700/month (full-time). Part-time workers typically earn RM 7–15/hour. At 20 hours/week, you could earn approximately RM 550–1,200/month — enough to cover a significant portion of your living expenses.

📌 How to Get Permission

  • Speak to your university's International Student Office
  • Submit a Part-Time Employment Approval Form to the Immigration Department
  • Wait for written approval before starting work
  • Keep all documents accessible at your place of work

Malaysia Student Visa Guide 2026: Everything You Need to Know

📅 Apr 20, 2026 ✍️ EduPath Visa Team ⏱ 7 min read

Getting your Malaysian student visa can feel overwhelming — EMGS, eVAL, Student Pass, VAL... the terminology alone is confusing. This guide cuts through the jargon and gives you a clear, step-by-step understanding of the entire process.

📌 Important: Malaysia's student visa process is managed by EMGS (Education Malaysia Global Services) — a government-authorized body that processes all international student applications. Your university cannot bypass EMGS.

📋 Step-by-Step Visa Process

  • Step 1 — Get Your Offer Letter: Your university issues a conditional or unconditional offer letter after reviewing your academic documents
  • Step 2 — Medical Screening: Undergo a medical check-up at an EMGS-approved clinic in your home country (or arrive on a Social Visit Pass first)
  • Step 3 — EMGS Application: Your university submits your eVAL (Electronic Visa Approval Letter) application through the EMGS portal
  • Step 4 — Pay EMGS Fees: Typically RM 1,200–2,500 depending on nationality and program duration
  • Step 5 — Receive eVAL: Processing takes 4–8 weeks. The eVAL is sent to your university email
  • Step 6 — Travel to Malaysia: Enter on your eVAL and present it at the airport immigration counter
  • Step 7 — Convert to Student Pass: Your university converts the eVAL to a full Student Pass/VAL within 30 days of arrival

📄 Required Documents

  • Valid passport (minimum 18 months validity)
  • Academic transcripts and certificates (original + certified translations)
  • IELTS/TOEFL or English proficiency proof (if applicable)
  • Passport-sized photographs
  • Medical examination results from an approved clinic
  • Financial proof (bank statements showing minimum RM 20,000–30,000)
  • Police Clearance Certificate

⏱ How Long Does It Take?

  • Simple cases (ASEAN students): 4–6 weeks
  • Complex cases (Middle East, South Asia): 6–12 weeks
  • Expedited processing (extra fee): 2–4 weeks
